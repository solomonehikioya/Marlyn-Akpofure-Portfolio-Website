Get to Know Me

Passionate about elevating the status of women in International Politics and Diplomacy

Marlyn has a degree in law and works as a professional in international relations and foreign policy. With more than ten years’ experience in foreign policy analysis, rapporteuring roundtable discussions, seminars, and brainstorming on global issues, including, but not limited to, terrorism, oil and gas/downstream analysis, women's development, food security, gender equality, migration, and, most recently, communications..

She has collaborated with the Olusegun Obasanjo Presidential Library and Africa Progress Group on a project aimed at leveraging Africa's population. In her current role as PR & Protocol Officer in the Office of the Director General at the Nigerian Institute of International Affairs, she keeps in tandem with International Politics, International Organizations, International Protocols, Public Relations, media and information dissemination worldwide while interfacing various Multinational organizations and Individuals of various strata in society. Also involved in Academic Research, she writes and presents papers on current International issues at the NIIA as well as for Distinguished Professors and Upstream Facilitators. In the course of her career, she has debriefed past Presidents, Foreign Ministers, Ambassadors, and top Military Officials on Foreign Policy Issues during their service. Marlyn has over 16 years combined experience in Domestic Law Practice, Branding and Marketing Communications, Business Development, Foreign Policy Analysis, globally accepted Protocular Diplomatic Practice and recently Research Methodology & Grant Writing. She has a degree in Law and a Master’s in Public and International Affairs.
